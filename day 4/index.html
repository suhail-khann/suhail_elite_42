    DES (Data Encryption Standard) is a symmetric block cipher that encrypts data in 64-bit blocks using a 56-bit key. The encryption process involves 16 rounds of permutation and substitution based on the Feistel structure. DES Encryption Steps: Initial Permutation: The 64-bit plaintext is permuted using a fixed table. Feistel Rounds: The 64-bit block is split into two halves, and 16 rounds of Feistel functions are applied. Final Permutation: After 16 rounds, a final permutation is applied to produce the ciphertext.
